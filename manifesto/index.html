The Manifesto

The new standard for legal AI

The legal industry is the most leverage-rich, least optimized business on earth. I'm here to fix that.

The old way is broken

For a century, legal work has been priced by the hour — a model that quietly punishes anyone who gets faster. The reward goes to friction: the longer the intake, the slower the draft, the bigger the invoice. So the busywork never gets fixed. It gets billed.

Meanwhile the way clients find lawyers changed completely. They ask ChatGPT and Perplexity before they ever touch Google. They judge a firm by its first landing page and its last short-form video. The firms winning today aren't the oldest or the loudest — they're the ones with the best systems.

What I believe

  • AI is not a gimmick or a threat. It's an operating system for revenue.
  • Busywork is the enemy — not headcount, not hours, not your team.
  • Visibility, workflow, and audience are one system, not three vendors.
  • You deserve the unvarnished truth about what to build and what to kill.
  • Results are the only currency that matters. Adjectives don't bill.

How I work

I don't sell hours and I don't sell hype. I find where the leverage is hiding in your practice — the intake that leaks clients, the page that doesn't convert, the search you're invisible in — and I engineer the system that fixes it. Then I make it compound.

I've done it inside one company already: as CTO of Amphs AI, I rebuilt the workflows that took the business from $14k to $60k a month. Same team. Better systems. That's the entire thesis.

The result

You stop managing busywork and start compounding leverage. The firm runs faster, gets found first, and bills for outcomes — not for time lost to admin.
